Fragments of the Digital Self

Exhibition curated by Maria Callanan

Curatorial Statement

This exhibition explores how early net artists used the internet as a space for constructing, fragmenting, and questioning identity. Rather than presenting identity as fixed or stable, these works reveal it as fluid, performative, and shaped by digital systems. Created primarily in the late 1990s and early 2000s, these projects reflect a moment when the internet was becoming a public, participatory space.

Olia Lialina’s My Boyfriend Came Back From the War uses hyperlink structures to break narrative into emotional fragments, mirroring the instability of memory and relationships. Similarly, Jennifer Ringley’s JenniCam transforms everyday life into a continuous digital performance, questioning boundaries between public and private identity. Finally, Eva and Franco Mattes’ No Fun critiques online spectatorship, exposing how digital audiences engage with identity as spectacle rather than reality.

Together, these works demonstrate how net art challenged traditional ideas of authorship, narrative, and selfhood. By using interactivity, live broadcasting, and performative gestures, these artists reveal how identity online is not simply expressed, but actively constructed and consumed within digital culture.

Artworks

My Boyfriend Came Back From the War (1996)

Olia Lialina

A pioneering net artwork that uses a series of clickable frames to tell a fragmented story about love and conflict. The piece mimics early web design while exploring how digital interfaces shape emotional storytelling.

JenniCam (1996–2003)

Jennifer Ringley

One of the first lifecasting projects, JenniCam broadcast the artist’s life 24/7. It challenged ideas of privacy, surveillance, and self-representation in the early internet era.

No Fun (2010)

Eva and Franco Mattes

A performance staged on Chatroulette in which Franco Mattes pretends to hang himself while users react live. The work critiques online spectatorship and emotional detachment in digital spaces.
